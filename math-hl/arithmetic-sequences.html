Problem 1 — Basic terms and sum
Given an arithmetic sequence with first term \(a_1 = 5\) and common difference \(d = 3\), find (a) the 20th term, and (b) the sum of the first 20 terms.
(a) nth term: display as
\[a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d = 5 + 19\cdot 3 = 62.\] (b) Sum of first \(n\) terms: \(S_n = \dfrac{n(a_1 + a_n)}{2}\). For \(n=20\):
\[S_{20} = 20\cdot\frac{5 + 62}{2} = 10\cdot 67 = 670.\]

Problem 2 — Determine \(a_1\) and \(d\) from sums
The sum of the first 10 terms is \(S_{10}=150\) and the sum of the first 20 terms is \(S_{20}=420\). Find \(a_1\) and \(d\).
Use \(S_n=\dfrac{n}{2}\bigl(2a_1+(n-1)d\bigr)\). \[S_{10}=5\bigl(2a_1+9d\bigr)=150\Rightarrow 2a_1+9d=30 \quad(1)\] \[S_{20}=10\bigl(2a_1+19d\bigr)=420\Rightarrow 2a_1+19d=42 \quad(2)\] Subtracting (2)-(1): \(10d=12\Rightarrow d=1.2\). Then \(2a_1+9(1.2)=30\Rightarrow a_1=9.6\).
Problem 3 — Smallest \(n\) for a sum bound
Find the smallest positive integer \(n\) such that the sum of the first \(n\) terms of the arithmetic sequence with \(a_1=8\) and \(d=-0.5\) is less than \(50\).
\(S_n=\dfrac{n}{2}\bigl(2a_1+(n-1)d\bigr)=\dfrac{n}{2}\bigl(16+(n-1)(-0.5)\bigr)\). We need \(S_n<50\). Simplify and solve the inequality; one gets the quadratic \[-0.5n^2+16.5n-100<0\] which leads to \(n^2-33n+200>0\) with roots \(n=8\) and \(n=25\). The inequality holds for \(n>25\), so the smallest integer is \(n=26\).
