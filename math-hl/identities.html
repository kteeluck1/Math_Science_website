Problem 1 — Prove the following identity
(a) \u00A0 \u00A0 \u00A0 \u00A0 \u00A0 sec \u03B8 / (sec \u03B8 + tan \u03B8) + tan \u03B8 / (sec \u03B8 - tan \u03B8) = 1 + 2 tan2 \u03B8
Combine the two fractions:
(sec^2\u00A0\u03B8 + tan^2\u00A0\u03B8) / (sec^2\u00A0\u03B8 - tan^2\u00A0\u03B8). Use the identity sec^2\u00A0\u03B8 = 1 + tan^2\u00A0\u03B8 to get
(1 + 2 tan^2\u00A0\u03B8) / 1 = 1 + 2 tan^2\u00A0\u03B8, which proves the result.
